Oh, The Places I've Lived!

I've lived many places during my fairly short lifetime, I will describe a few of those places here.

Wiesbaden, DE

I was born in Wiesbadden Germany on Janurary 5, 1990. I was too young to remember living here, so it is now on my list of places to travel. Everyone I've met that has been tells me how amazing it is.

San Antonio, FL

My grandparents raised me in this small town. At the time the population was about 800. This is also where my mother and her two sisters were raised. Pictured below is St.Anthony's catholic school at the heart of the town. My mother, her two sisters, my older brother and I all attended this school. To me, it always felt a wee bit haunted.

Gerhardsbrunn, DE

Gerhardsbrunn Germany is by far the most beautiful place I have lived. The landscape, architecture, and lifestyle is something I had not and have not since incountered. It is such a small village that I had trouble finding an image. Every few days I would walk to the dairy, toss my three € in a cup, and fill our jugs with fresh milk straight from the silo. The town was always calm. When I walked the streets I would see cows, horses, goats, and the residents tending there beautiful gardens. During the fall the fields would be overflowing with pumpkins. When it snowed it was always the perfect amount, I could build a respectable snowman while not worrying about shoveling the drive. The summers were seldom hot, but when it was it was a dry heat that was hard to bare having no A/C, but it was worth it.

Macungie, PA

I moved to Macungie Pensylvania on a whim. After eves dropping on a conversation about a nanny job and decided to jump on the oprotunity. Two days later I was on the road to PA with a nice older man I had just met. Probably not the best decision I've made now that I think about it. Macungie is suburbia, filled with potholes and soccer moms. I took care of three children aged 5, 7, and 9. We lived on a culdesac and the weather was pretty nice until winter. Shoveling snow is not fun. Other than that the best thing about living here was being so close to New York City.

Boca Raton, FL

After my stint as a live in nanny I made the move to South Florida and landed in Boca Raton. Boca Raton Florida is beautiful and bugiee. The beaches are gorgous, the city is clean, and the people have their dogs in carriges and kids on leashes. I never planned on staying long, but ended up getting a seasonal part time job-turned 5 year mini career. Oops.
